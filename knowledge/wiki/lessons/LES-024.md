- Category: process (lesson consumed) / self-found-defect (the live roadmap drift is a real, still-open bug the detector surfaced).
- Lesson: state-file claims are cheaply verifiable against ground truth. Two robust signals cover the observed failure modes — (1) an early-phase roadmap label paired with completion markers in CURRENT_STATE, and (2) newest merged PR in `git log` vs the newest PR referenced in the state docs. Tune conservatively: exit non-zero (and surface a guardian WARN) only on HARD drift; the normal one-PR reconciliation lag must stay SOFT/quiet so the check never cries wolf. Keep it the deterministic floor of a two-tier design — new signals are added as pure `check_*` functions, and a reasoned tier could later explain/prioritise drift behind the stable `Finding` seam.
- Loop feed: consumed by AOS-20. The detector's live HARD finding on `.archetype/roadmap.md` is a true positive whose *fix* is owned by the pending doc-reconciliation/sync PR (out of AOS-20's scope). The guardian WARN now makes that drift visible on every PR until reconciled — a forcing function, mirroring LES-009.
