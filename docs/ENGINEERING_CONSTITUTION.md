# Engineering Constitution

## Preamble

ArchetypeOS exists to improve engineering decision making. It is an engineering reasoning platform before it is a build tool.

## Article I — Purpose

The purpose of ArchetypeOS is to increase engineering quality. Success is measured by the quality of systems created, not the volume of code produced.

## Article II — Research Before Implementation

Significant recommendations should be grounded in research. Preferred sources include official documentation, reference implementations, architecture patterns, security guidance, benchmarks, and clearly labeled community experience.

## Article III — Evidence Over Opinion

Recommendations must be based on verifiable evidence. When evidence is missing, the system must say so.

## Article IV — Fitness Over Familiarity

Technology choices must not be made only because a developer already knows the tool. Familiarity may influence rollout planning, but it must not override engineering fitness.

## Article V — Architecture Is a First-Class Artifact

Architecture should exist before, during, and after implementation. Major systems should maintain architecture graphs, dependency maps, data flow, trust boundaries, and decision history.

## Article VI — Every Decision Has Memory

Every significant decision should record context, alternatives, evidence, reasoning, consequences, date, version, and approval.

## Article VII — Explainability

Every recommendation must explain why it exists, what alternatives were considered, what tradeoffs were accepted, what risks remain, and how confidence was determined.

## Article VIII — Verification Before Trust

When deterministic verification exists, it should be preferred over inference. Agents should use code, tests, static analysis, dependency inspection, configuration, documentation, and security tooling before relying only on language models.

## Article IX — Human Authority

AI assists. Humans decide. Actions that materially alter software, infrastructure, security posture, or production environments require explicit human approval unless intentionally delegated.

## Article X — Continuous Evolution

No recommendation is permanent. Technologies mature, architectures evolve, and security changes. ArchetypeOS should re-evaluate prior decisions against current evidence.

## Article XI — Modular Intelligence

Engines, agents, models, providers, research sources, and knowledge systems must be replaceable without redesigning the whole platform.

## Article XII — Engineering Integrity

Unknowns must remain unknown until evidence exists. Metrics and confidence scores must not be manipulated to support predetermined conclusions.
