## Content

- Event: the accepted-warnings mechanism (AOS-PRG-003, built from LES-006) worked exactly as designed. LES-006 established that a warning which never changes behavior is invisible; the fix was to make deferral explicit and dated, not silent. That dated deferral — `review_by: 2026-08-01` — then acted as a forcing function: rather than the web-test gap drifting indefinitely, the looming escalation-to-BLOCK put AOS-WEB-001 at the front of Sprint 5.
- Source: PR #42 → this package (AOS-WEB-001).
- Category: process
- Lesson: a conscious, dated acceptance of a warning is not just documentation — the expiry date is a scheduling commitment. It converts "we will get to this" into a hard deadline that the roadmap must honor or consciously renew. This is the intended, healthy behavior of the accepted-warnings registry.
- Loop feed: the mechanism is validated in production use; no change needed. Future deferred-work acceptances should likewise carry a `review_by` that the sprint planning treats as a real deadline.
- Status: closed — consumed by this package: the acceptance was retired (registry set to `[]`) by real Playwright tests plus guardian enforcement (`web-tests-not-enforced` now fires only when `apps/web/src/` changes without `apps/web/e2e/` changes), rather than renewed.
